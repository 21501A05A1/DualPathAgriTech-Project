Temperate Fruits

Temperate Fruits can tolerate both diurnal and seasonal wide fluctuation of temperature and are grown only in place where winter is distinctly cold.

  • Common in four-season regions
  • Many types suitable for home gardens
  • Moderate climate requirements
  • Varieties include apples, pears, and peaches
  • Require cross-pollination for optimal fruit set

Meditarean Fruits

Mediterranean fruits thrive in regions with a Mediterranean climate .

  • Fruit varieties that are adapted to the Mediterranean climate, such as figs, olives, citrus fruits,pomegranates, and grapes
  • Planting in the Mediterranean region often occurs in the fall or early winter.
  • Well-draining soil is crucial to prevent root rot, especially during the rainy season.
